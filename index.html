Clinical Psychology
for Children, Teens and Young Adults
in the Tigard Triangle near I-5
off 72nd Ave. at 217/Kruse Way
or by Call

Portland Psychological Services is operated by C. David Maxey, M.A., Licensed Independent Psychologist Associate (Oregon Board of Psychology License 5067). He specializes in Clinical Psychology for children, teens, and young adults (approximately ages 4 to 25) and has extensive experience providing mental health services to children and youth over the past {{ site.time | date: "%Y" | minus: 2001 }} years. His evidence-based approach to therapy is individualized to each client’s needs, rather than following a step-by-step manual-based approach. He incorporates scientifically supported treatment modalities, including Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT), Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT), Trauma-Focused Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (TF-CBT), Parent Management Training (PMT), and Collaborative and Proactive Solutions (formerly known as Collaborative Problem Solving).

New Clients

During the school year, I offer after-school appointments, which I'm able to provide by working during the school day as the Director of Student Support at a K-8 public charter school, which is separate from my private practice work. If you’d like to discuss my services and how they might fit with your needs, schedule a phone call during my mid-day block reserved for calls and asynchronous work. Alternatively, you can call 503‑928‑4182 at your convenience to leave a voicemail for a callback. I usually return calls on the same or next day between 9am and 7pm, excluding weekends and holidays.

In-Network Insurance

Portland Psychological Services is in-network with the following insurance companies.

If you have a different insurance, you can receive a receipt (sometimes called a “superbill”) that you can submit to your insurance to use out‑of‑network benefits.

Fees

Fees for psychotherapy are $250 for the first appointment and $150 for regular 45-minute appointments. When using in-network insurance, the contracted rates may be slightly lower and can be reviewed with you upon request.

FSA and HSA cards may be accepted for some fees.

Keeping a credit card stored on file is required. Card payments are accepted for the first and regular appointments with an additional $5 convenience fee, which can be avoided by paying at the time of service with cash, check, or (sent through your bank’s app to {{site.email.font}}).
