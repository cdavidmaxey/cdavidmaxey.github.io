C. David Maxey, M.A.
Licensed Psychologist Associate

Clinical Child & Adolescent
Psychotherapy and Psychological Evaluation
in the Tigard Triangle near I-5
on 72nd Avenue at Kruse Way

I specialize in Clinical Child and Adolescent Psychology, and my approach is rooted in using assessment to thoughtfully select evidence-based treatments, including Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT), Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT), behavior management, collaborative problem-solving, and others. I invite you to talk with me about your needs and my services, so we can find out if we might be a good fit. The steps below outline the process.

New Client Process

★My online forms and file uploader are powered by a special Microsoft account, HIPAA-secured with a Business Associate Agreement to protect the privacy of your health information.

1

New Client Request

Complete the online New Client Request★ form to provide some basic information.

2

Brief Phone Call

I will contact you to schedule an appointment for a free 10-20 minute phone call, so we can briefly discuss followup questions to help us both decide if your needs and my services might be a good fit.

3

Forms

Complete the Regular Mental Health Services forms, unless you are using EAP (Employee Assistance Program) benefits from your employer—you need to provide an EAP Authorization Code from your insurance to use EAP benefits.

Regular Mental Health Services
  • PDF Parent Questionnaire
    It’s fine for only one parent to complete this questionnaire, and submit the PDF through my online file uploader★ or by email to {{site.email}}. email may not be a private way to communicate.
  • Online Parent Questionnaire
    One or both parents can complete this questionnaire easily using a phone, tablet, or computer.
  • Online Youth Questionnaire (for ages 11 years old and older)
    It’s helpful if youth 11 years old and older fill out this questionnaire, which can be completed easily using a phone, tablet, or computer. If anything is too hard, too long, too much, or whatever, please skip some or all of it.
EAP (Employee Assistance Program) Services
  • PDF Parent Questionnaire
    It’s fine for only one parent to complete this questionnaire, and submit the PDF through my online file uploader★ or by email to {{site.email}}. email may not be a private way to communicate.
  • Online Parent Questionnaire
    One or both parents can complete this questionnaire easily using a phone, tablet, or computer.
  • Online Youth Questionnaire (for ages 11 years old and older)
    It’s helpful if youth 11 years old and older fill out this questionnaire, which can be completed easily using a phone, tablet, or computer. If anything is too hard, too long, too much, or whatever, please skip some or all of it.
4

Agreement

Please review the Agreement before our first appointment. The Agreement is signed by parents and youth 14 years old or older. At the beginning of the first appointment, we will review the Agreement and any questions you have. Before we move on with the rest of the first appointment, the signed Agreement is submitted as a PDF through my online file uploader★ or by email to {{site.email}}. email may not be a private way to communicate.

5

Appointment

Regular Mental Health Services start with a diagnostic evaluation, typically leading to a full course of treatment or evaluation.

First Appointment

The first appointment is usually up to 90-120 minutes, scheduled during our phone call, and for meeting only with parents—children do not come to the first appointment. This appointment will be automatically cancelled and may not be rescheduled, if the parent forms (listed in step 3) are not completed by 8am on the day before this appointment.

Second Appointment

The second appointment is 45 minutes, scheduled during our phone call or the first appointment, and for meeting with your child. Use my online scheduler★ to request a 45-minute appointment**

EAP (Employee Assistance Program) Services are for brief psychotherapy (typically involving five or fewer appointments) to clarify problems, develop a plan to resolve them, work toward problem resolution, and if needed, refer you to additional services with me or another professional--EAP services do not involve making a diagnosis and start with 45-minute counseling appointments.

First Appointment

The first appointment is 45 minutes, scheduled during our phone call, and for meeting only with parents for at least the beginning of the appointment—your child may join later in the first appointment or the next appointment. This appointment will be automatically cancelled and may not be rescheduled, if the parent forms (listed in step 3) are not completed by 8am on the day before this appointment.
