New EAP Client Process

EAP (Employee Assistance Program) services are for brief psychotherapy, usually limited to about five appointments. This typically involves clarifying problems, developing a plan to resolve them, working toward problem resolution, and if needed, referring you to additional services with me or another professional—EAP services do not involve making a diagnosis. You need to provide an EAP Authorization Code from your insurance or EAP to use EAP benefits, which cover the full cost of services.

2

3

To-Do List to Schedule First Appointment

The first appointment is scheduled after you complete the To-Do List items.

  • Upload or email Records or Reports from other providers (if you have any to share).
    When sending copies of papers, please properly scan them with a scanner or app of your choice (e.g., Microsoft Lens on iOS or Android).
  • Upload or email the Developmental History (Fillable) PDF
    It’s fine for only one parent to complete the Developmental History.
    When sending copies of papers, please properly scan them with a scanner or app of your choice (e.g., Microsoft Lens on iOS or Android).
  • Online Parent Questionnaire
    One or more parents can complete this questionnaire.
  • Online Youth Questionnaire for ages 11 years old and older
4

Form to Sign

After I receive the items listed above, I’ll arrange to schedule the first appointment and send a form to sign—look for an email with a link to sign online.

5

Appointments

(in-office or by video call)

First Appointment 45 minutes

for meeting only with parents—children do not come to the first appointment

Second Appointment 45 minutes

primarily for meeting your child

Subsequent Appointments 45 minutes

for meeting with parents, your child, or both, based on your child’s needs
