Child & Adolescent Specialty
With a Focus on Specific Needs

I specialize in services for youth who have one or more of the following related kinds of needs:

  • Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD): difficulties with concentration, organization, and planning
  • Autism with Support Needs Met by Weekly or Every-Other-Week Therapy: Autistic youth whose needs fit well with my services usually need only accommodations in school or up to about an hour per week with a special education teacher in the learning center (i.e., do not need a specialized classroom).
  • Emotional Disorders: Panic Attacks, Phobias, Depression, and Generalized, Social, or Separation Anxiety
  • Sleep Problems including those caused by problematic internet use
  • Dysregulation, Inflexibility, or Defiance, which can arise from struggles with any of the above
  • Tics & Body-Focused Repetitive Behaviors (e.g., hair-pulling, nail-biting, and skin-picking)

I can briefly explain that my services do not fit well with legal or custody issues, serious substance use problems, complex trauma, eating disorders, autism requiring substantial supports (e.g., intensive therapies, ABA, etc.), or youth who cannot tolerate attending sessions at my office or on . Those needs are best served by other specialties, usually within a large multi-disiplinary team.

Schedule a free call to talk with me about your child's needs to decide if they fit with my services.

Experienced Professional
You Can Get to Know and Trust

Simply put, I offer personalized care and attention, a refreshing contrast to large group practices where your child can feel like they’re visiting a clinic. When you work with me, you’re just “going to see David”, a familiar face you’ve come to know and trust.

Schedule a free call and you'll talk with me, not a client intake coordinator.

Prioritizing Skill‑Building & Improvements
To Get Your Child Out of Therapy

Therapy and psychological evaluation can be great investments in your child’s future, particularly when the goal is building skills to put your child on a path to thriving and no longer needing therapy. I definitely get to know my clients well, but we’re not deeply exploring your child’s inner self for years and years—there are people who can do that with your child; that’s just not me.
