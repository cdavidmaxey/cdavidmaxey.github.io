If You See Me Online or Around Town

Policy on Social Settings

Online social networks (like Facebook and Twitter) are neither professional contexts nor private ways to communicate. Therefore, I do not use them to interact with people I serve professionally. If you happen to see me online, please do not contact, friend, or follow me in any way. If I am contacted, I will ignore, block, or not reply to that contact. If I discover that I have accidentally connected with you, I will cancel or block that connection. Please understand that I do not interact with you online to protect your child’s and family’s privacy and to maintain our professional relationship.

If I see you around town, I will act as if I don’t know you because you may not want to explain why you know me to others with you, with me, or nearby. If you approach me, I will usually say a brief hello, but I urge you to consider whether that might be uncomfortable for your child or family. It is not a time for us to discuss professional matters. Please remember that unless you approach me, I will act as if I don’t know you to protect your child’s and family’s privacy.

Please do not write online reviews or post about me on your social networks. Mental health providers cannot respond online because we are bound to protect confidentiality. Instead please contact me directly. Please appreciate that online reviews and posts pose risks to your child’s and family’s privacy now and in the future as information on the internet tends to live on forever and can be connected to you, even if you wish to remain anonymous.
